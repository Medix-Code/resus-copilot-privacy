Resus Copilot · Privacy Policy

How Resus Copilot handles app data

This policy applies to the Resus Copilot mobile app (package com.medixcode.resuscopilot). It is intended to meet Google Play requirements and explain, in specific terms, what data is stored, where it is stored, and when it may leave the device.

Short version

Resus Copilot does not require account registration, does not use analytics or advertising SDKs, and does not automatically send session data to a developer-operated server. Session information is stored on the device and only leaves the device if you choose to export or share it.

Data the app may process

Category Examples Purpose and location
Session and operational log data Internal session ID, start/end time, selected protocol, weight, age, rhythm changes, shocks, medications, procedures, free-text notes and session outcome. Used for timing, logging, summaries and exports. Stored locally on the device in an encrypted database. Not automatically transmitted to the developer.
Settings and legal records Language, volume, alerts, export preferences, and accepted legal consent version/date. Used to preserve app settings and record which legal notice version you accepted. Stored locally and encrypted.
Notification permission and state Whether notifications are enabled and the minimal counters needed to render an ongoing-session notification. Used for cycle reminders, persistent notifications and similar OS-level session features. Processed on-device through the operating system.
Exported files PDF or TXT reports created from the app. Created locally when you tap export, save or share. If you send a file to another app, cloud service or destination, that third party will handle the file under its own policy.

What the app does not do

  • It does not create user accounts in a first-party backend.
  • It does not include analytics, commercial tracking or ad SDKs.
  • It does not sell or share data for advertising purposes.
  • It is not designed to access camera, microphone, contacts or location.

The app also does not require direct patient identifiers such as a name or record number. However, free-text notes and exported reports may contain sensitive information if you choose to enter it. Avoid entering direct identifiers unless your internal protocol expressly allows it.

Permissions and related features

Notifications

Resus Copilot may request notification permission in order to show reminders, audio alerts and an ongoing-session notification. These notifications are generated on the device and support the core timer workflow.

Export and sharing

When you export or share a report, the file is created in a local app directory or the destination you choose. If you then send it through another app or cloud service, that transfer is governed by the recipient you selected, not by Resus Copilot.

Security and storage

Local records and settings are stored in an encrypted on-device database. The encryption key is kept in secure platform storage when available.

No technical measure guarantees zero risk. If you export files or share screenshots, you must also follow your institution's security rules and any clinical confidentiality requirements that apply to you.

Retention and deletion

  • Session history and settings remain on the device until you delete them in the app, clear app data, or uninstall the app.
  • Exported files remain in the local folder, Downloads, or the destination you selected until you or that destination deletes them.
  • The app may offer optional local cleanup of older exported files.

Your choices and controls

  • Review, export or delete saved sessions.
  • Disable notifications in the app or system settings.
  • Withdraw legal consent from settings.
  • Delete the app or its data to remove local storage.

Because the app does not maintain a central user account or a copy of your session data on developer servers, access, portability and erasure are primarily exercised on your own device.

Recipients and third parties

Resus Copilot does not share clinical or session data with third parties by default. Third parties may only receive data if you explicitly choose them when exporting or sharing a report, or when the operating system provides notification, storage or sharing services needed to complete your action.

Intended audience

The app is intended for healthcare professionals or users with appropriate training. It does not replace clinical judgment, institutional protocols or official emergency systems.

Privacy inquiries

You can submit privacy-related questions or issues through the public privacy-policy tracker: github.com/Medix-Code/resus-copilot-privacy/issues/new.

Do not post patient names, clinical identifiers, care documents or sensitive health information in a public issue.
